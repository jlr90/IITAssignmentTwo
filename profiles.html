    Meet the team

    For individual profiles and summaries of the members of GS Technologies, please refer to the tab navigation below.

    In reference to personality tests taken by each of the group members, there is a high proportion of natural leaders in the group. Instead of being a point of conflict however, the subtle differences in style and approach between the team members complement one another. Some members are able to excel in areas where others may find difficulties.

    All members are ‘team players’, in that they are easy-going, friendly and most importantly, supportive. Everyone is able to organise themselves effectively and communicate their intentions and ideas to the group. This ensures nobody falls behind and everyone can be confident in each other’s ability to handle the workload.

    According to the test results, Helen and John both approach tasks logically and analytically. This is balanced by Ben’s results which indicate he approaches tasks with more creativity and can think about things in a more abstract sense. Both Andrew and Sam are adept at noticing problems when they arise and coming up with solutions.

    An important consideration in any group is not just how the individual member’s skills complement one another, but in how the group operates as a cohesive unit. Sam, Andrew and John all display traits of calmness and cooperativeness in their Big-Five Personality tests. Combined with Ben’s skills in understanding and communication, and Helen’s skills in motivation and communication, the five group members are easily able to work with one another and avoid any conflict. This enables the group as a whole to avoid any pitfalls that might be common in group assignment situations. Some of these may include; one or more members falling behind in their work, or a clash of ideas between stubborn, leader type personalities.

    No particular member is a standout at detail-oriented tasks; however, this is also not necessarily a weakness for any member either. Given the strong communication and understanding between members, the group members are able and ready to assist one another where necessary.

    Group Reflection

    "Working with Group 7 has been, for the most part, an unexpected delight. There has been a theme of helpfulness and everybody is keen to do their bit. A wide range of skill sets from the members has been hugely beneficial. Andew and Helen jumped in early on to set up some of the things we'd need to work effectively (Discord channel for communications, Smartsheets for interactive checklists to allow for a clear overview of what needs to happen and who's assigned to the tasks). John, with his experience of HTML/CSS/front end coding and GitHub, has been able to take the lead on website design, and has been assisting us with how to approach both. Sam had the time to leap straight into the assignment content.

    The team overall has been very responsive and helpful where required. Everyone is keen to work together, and everyone's want to do well by the assignment and by each other has been lovely. In the lead up to the assessment deadline, we had agreed to split the workload contributions percentage evenly. I’d suggested the idea as it was a simple way of acknowledging that we’d all been contributing, and I felt it would be good for a sense of comradery and trust for the group going forward. I noticed that shortly after this was decided, the due diligence and attention to detail in the preparation of certain tasks dropped significantly. The drive for some members to see their tasks through to polished completion waned. I am not sure this is a cause and effect (if certain members felt they no longer needed to maintain quality, trusting they would now receive similar assignment marks regardless of their input), or if this should be attributed toward something else (perhaps burnout coming toward the end of a project). Either way, I did notice a last minute dip in quality and a disinterest in following through on fine-tuning from some group members.

    The group had initially started out with a sixth, Chris Brissett who confirmed on 17/7/20 that he would no longer be able to continue with this project. Chris had become unresponsive during the course of the project, but eventually did get in touch to let us know that he was no longer able to continue with the task. Because the rest of the team has been working diligently and cohesively, Chris' absence has thankfully not made a strong impact on the effectiveness of the team. "
